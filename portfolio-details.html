Here’s a well‑structured paragraph summarizing IoT and gas leakage detection : IoT‑based gas leakage detection systems combine sensitive gas sensors (commonly MQ‑series such as MQ‑2, MQ‑5 or MQ‑6) with microcontrollers like ESP32 or ESP8266 to continuously monitor concentrations of LPG, methane, carbon monoxide and other hazardous gases. When a sensor reading exceeds a predefined threshold, the microcontroller triggers instantaneous local alarms (buzzers, LEDs) and sends real‑time notifications to cloud platforms or mobile devices. More advanced systems can automatically actuate shut‑off valves to isolate the gas supply and even activate exhaust fans to disperse leaked gas ([hashstudioz.com][1]). Data is logged to the cloud for remote monitoring, historical analysis, and predictive insights using AI or TinyML analytics to distinguish between false alarms and true leaks ([knowhowcommunity.org], [hashstudioz.com]). These intelligent systems support scalability—from single‑household setups to industrial installations integrating multiple sensors across a site—with centralized dashboard control, trend analysis, and faster intervention ([viact.ai], [knowhowcommunity.org]). Real‑world deployments have shown this approach enhances safety, minimizes accident risk, optimizes fuel use, and offers early alerts before issues escalate ([thespruce.com]).
